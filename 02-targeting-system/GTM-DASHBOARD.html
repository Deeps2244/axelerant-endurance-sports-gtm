🎯 Typical Deal Approval Flow
1. Discovery Trigger — VP Ops/CTO identifies pain point (registration outage, M&A complexity, scaling wall)
2. Internal Alignment — VP Ops socializes with Event Director + CFO (operationally feasible? Financially justified?)
3. Confirmation — Event Director validates athlete experience problem, CFO reviews budget impact
4. Proposal & Negotiation — VP Ops requests formal proposals (usually 2–3 vendors), CFO leads evaluation
5. Committee Decision — Executive sponsor (CEO or Board for non-profits) approves scope + budget
6. Project Launch — Event Director becomes internal champion; VP Ops oversees execution; CFO tracks ROI
🏆 How We Create Internal Advocates
Early Phase: Identify who's most motivated to fix the problem (usually Event Director or VP Ops). Make them our primary contact.
Middle Phase: Activate them as an internal advocate during vendor evaluation. They validate our fit to internal stakeholders.
Late Phase: Leverage them to unblock CFO concerns. They provide operational data that proves ROI case.
Post-Sale: Make them the project success owner. Their win is visible to the entire organization.
